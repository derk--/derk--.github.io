I am an Associate Professor of Computer Science and Engineering at Wright State University and am part of the Kno.e.sis Research Center. My research interests are in developing statistical network models, deep learning systems, and modern clustering methods to understand and make sense of data generated from cyber-physical or cyber-logical networks and systems. Application areas of interest include Web systems analysis, geospatial processes, computational learning methods, and social computing. My research is supported by NSF, AFRL, ORISE, and the Ohio Federal Research Network.

Recent news an updates about my research, scholarship, and my student's accomplishments can be found at the homepage of the Web and Complex Systems Lab.

Before Wright State I was an Interim Researcher at ABB Corporate Research and a visitor of the Dependable Distributed Systems and Networks lab at National Taiwan University as an NSF EAPSI Fellow. I am also a twice summer alumnus of Bell Labs. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from the University of Connecticut.
